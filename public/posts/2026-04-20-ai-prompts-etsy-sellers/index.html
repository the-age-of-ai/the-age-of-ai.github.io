How to Write Better Etsy Product Descriptions with AI Prompts

April 20, 2026 · by Alan McCarthy

If you’re an Etsy seller, you know the grind of writing product descriptions. You’ve got a shop full of unique, handmade items, but turning their charm into words that actually sell? That’s a whole different beast. Most sellers slap together a few generic lines, hoping buyers will “get it.” Spoiler: they don’t. Weak descriptions mean fewer clicks, fewer sales, and a shop that’s stuck in the Etsy algorithm’s basement.

The good news? You don’t need to be a copywriting genius to fix this. AI tools like ChatGPT can help you crank out compelling, keyword-rich descriptions in minutes—if you know how to ask the right questions. That’s where prompts come in. In this post, I’m going to show you how to use AI to level up your Etsy listings, including a real, actionable prompt you can try today. And if you want a full arsenal of ideas, I’ve got something for you at the end.

Why Etsy Product Descriptions Matter (and Why Yours Might Suck)

Let’s be real: a bad description can tank even the best product. Buyers skim. They’re looking for reasons to click “Add to Cart” or bounce. If your listing reads like a boring catalog entry—“Handmade bracelet, 7 inches, silver”—you’re losing them. Etsy’s search algorithm also favors listings with detailed, keyword-packed descriptions. No keywords, no visibility.

The problem is time. You’re already juggling crafting, shipping, and customer messages. Writing 10 unique, persuasive descriptions a day feels impossible. That’s where AI steps in. It can’t replace your creativity, but it can do the heavy lifting, spitting out drafts you can tweak in seconds.

How AI Prompts Work for Etsy Sellers

AI prompts are just specific instructions you give a tool like ChatGPT to get the output you want. Think of it as hiring a junior copywriter who works for free and never sleeps. The trick is being clear and detailed in your ask. A vague prompt like “Write a product description” gets you generic garbage. A targeted prompt gets you gold.

Here’s a quick framework for crafting AI prompts for Etsy:

  1. Specify the product: Include details like materials, size, or unique features.
  2. Define the tone: Do you want playful, professional, or cozy vibes?
  3. Target the audience: Mention who the product is for (e.g., “boho brides” or “pet lovers”).
  4. Include keywords: Add Etsy search terms for SEO (e.g., “handmade leather wallet”).
  5. Set a goal: Tell the AI to focus on benefits, storytelling, or urgency.

A Free AI Prompt to Try Right Now

Let’s put this to work. Below is a prompt you can copy and paste into ChatGPT (or any similar tool) to generate a product description for your Etsy shop. I’m using a hypothetical handmade candle as an example, but you can swap in your own product details.

Prompt:
“Write a 100-word Etsy product description for a handmade lavender soy candle, 8 oz, with a 40-hour burn time. Use a warm, inviting tone for customers looking for self-care gifts. Highlight the calming scent and eco-friendly materials. Include keywords like ‘lavender soy candle,’ ‘handmade candle,’ and ‘self-care gift.’ Focus on the emotional benefits of relaxation and stress relief.”

Sample Output (what you might get from ChatGPT):
“Unwind with this handmade lavender soy candle, an 8 oz oasis of calm with a 40-hour burn time. Crafted with eco-friendly soy wax, it fills your space with a soothing lavender scent—perfect for stress relief after a long day. Ideal as a self-care gift or a personal treat, this handmade candle transforms any room into a sanctuary. Light it during a bath or quiet evening to melt away tension. Shop this lavender soy candle now and embrace relaxation! Hand-poured with love for your peace of mind.”

See how specific that is? It’s not perfect—you’ll want to tweak it to match your brand voice or add a personal story—but it’s a solid starting point. Run this prompt for each listing, adjust the details, and you’ve got descriptions done in half the time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid with AI Descriptions

Before you go prompt-crazy, let’s talk pitfalls. AI isn’t magic. If you’re not careful, you’ll end up with robotic or repetitive text that turns buyers off. Here’s what to watch for:

  • Overused phrases: AI loves clichés like “perfect for any occasion.” Edit those out.
  • Keyword stuffing: Don’t let SEO keywords make your description unreadable. Balance is key.
  • Lack of personality: AI won’t know your story. Add a line about why you made the product or what inspired it.
  • Ignoring Etsy rules: AI might suggest claims (like “best candle ever”) that violate Etsy’s policies. Double-check.

Fix these by treating AI output as a rough draft. Spend 2-3 minutes personalizing each description, and you’re golden.
