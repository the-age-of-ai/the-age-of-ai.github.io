How to Create Better Client Resources with AI for Coaches and Therapists

If you’re a coach or therapist, you know the grind of creating client resources. Worksheets, guided exercises, reflection prompts—these tools are essential for helping clients grow, but they take hours to brainstorm and write. You’re already stretched thin with sessions, admin, and marketing. How do you keep delivering high-quality materials without burning out?

AI can be your shortcut. It’s not about replacing your expertise; it’s about amplifying it. With the right prompts, AI tools like ChatGPT or Claude can help you generate ideas, draft content, and refine resources in a fraction of the time. I’m going to walk you through a practical way to do this, step by step, so you can start using AI today to support your clients better.

Why Client Resources Matter (and Why They’re So Hard to Make)

First, let’s be real: personalized resources can transform a client’s progress. A well-crafted worksheet or journaling prompt helps them internalize lessons between sessions. But crafting these takes deep thought—tailoring to specific struggles, ensuring the tone matches your style, and avoiding generic fluff. On top of that, you’ve got to keep producing fresh content to stay relevant. It’s no wonder so many coaches and therapists struggle to keep up.

AI can’t replace your insight into human behavior, but it can handle the heavy lifting of ideation and drafting. Let’s break down a process you can use right now to create a client resource—a guided reflection exercise—with AI.

Step-by-Step: Build a Guided Reflection Exercise with AI

Here’s a practical method to create a resource for your clients using AI. I’ll use ChatGPT as the example, but this works with most AI tools. If you don’t have a paid plan, the free version can still get you started.

1. Define the Goal and Audience

Before you touch AI, get clear on what you’re making and for whom. Let’s say you’re a life coach working with clients on overcoming self-doubt. Your goal is a reflection exercise to help them identify limiting beliefs. Write this down: “Reflection exercise for clients aged 30-45 struggling with self-doubt, focused on uncovering limiting beliefs.”

2. Craft a Specific Prompt

AI shines when you give it clear instructions. Don’t just say, “Write a worksheet.” Be detailed. Try this prompt:

I’m a life coach creating a reflection exercise for clients aged 30-45 who struggle with self-doubt. The goal is to help them identify limiting beliefs. Write a 5-question guided reflection with short instructions for each question. Keep the tone empathetic and encouraging, avoiding jargon. Include a brief intro (50 words) explaining the purpose of the exercise.

Copy-paste that into ChatGPT or your preferred tool. You’ll get a draft instantly.

3. Review and Personalize

AI output isn’t perfect. It might sound too generic or miss your unique voice. Read through the draft. Does the intro align with how you’d speak to a client? Are the questions actionable? Edit it. Add a personal anecdote or tweak the tone. For example, if the AI writes, “Reflect on a time you felt inadequate,” you might change it to, “Think back to a moment you felt like you weren’t enough—what was happening then?”

4. Test It

Before handing it to a client, try the exercise yourself or ask a trusted peer to review it. Does it feel helpful? If not, tweak the questions or go back to AI with feedback like, “Make question 3 more specific to career-related self-doubt.”

5. Format and Deliver

Turn the text into a clean PDF or Google Doc. Add your branding—logo, colors, whatever makes it yours. Now you’ve got a professional resource in under an hour.

What You’ll Gain from Using AI This Way

Using AI for client resources doesn’t just save time; it lets you scale your impact. You can create tailored exercises for different client needs without starting from scratch each time. Plus, you’ll free up mental space for what matters—connecting with clients and growing your practice.

That said, AI isn’t a magic wand. It can churn out fluff if your prompts are vague. And it won’t know your clients’ nuances like you do. Always review and inject your expertise. Think of it as a first draft machine, not a final product generator.

Bonus Tip: Batch Your Resource Creation

Don’t stop at one exercise. Use AI to brainstorm a series of resources in one sitting. Prompt it with, “Give me 10 ideas for worksheets on building self-confidence for therapy clients.” Pick the best ones, then generate drafts for each. Spend a couple of hours batching, and you’ll have a month’s worth of materials ready.
